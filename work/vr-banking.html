01

Brief

A major banking client wanted to explore the feasibility of a virtual branch — a fully spatial, VR-based banking experience that could serve customers without a physical branch network. The brief was open-ended by design: is this a concept worth pursuing? If so, what does it feel like to bank in three dimensions?

There was no prior VR banking UX reference in the Indian market. The entire design methodology had to be built from first principles.

02

My role

Lead UX designer and researcher — from concept sketches through to device-ready prototype and user testing sessions.

  • →Concept development and spatial interaction design principles
  • →Low-fidelity paper prototyping for spatial layout logic
  • →Digital wireframing adapted for the VR viewport and 3-DOF interaction model
  • →User testing coordination — participants tested on a consumer VR headset
  • →Research synthesis and client presentation
03

The challenge

Spatial UX operates under constraints that 2D screen design doesn't prepare you for. Field-of-view, depth perception, locomotion, and the cognitive load of a fully immersive environment all interact in ways that can't be simulated on a flat screen.

The primary challenge was information density: banking requires a lot of data — balances, transaction history, product terms — and presenting this spatially without overwhelming the user required rethinking every fundamental assumption about information layout.

04

What I drove

  • 01Developed a spatial information architecture framework for banking services
  • 02Designed the gaze-and-dwell interaction model for accessibility-first VR interaction
  • 03Built three prototype iterations — paper storyboard, 2D wireframe, functional VR prototype
  • 04Ran user testing sessions with diverse participant profiles — tech-savvy and first-time VR users
  • 05Delivered findings and a recommended VR UX design system for future banking spatial experiences
05

The outcome

  • →Successfully validated that core banking transactions (balance check, fund transfer, product enquiry) are feasible in a VR environment
  • →Identified three critical failure modes — spatial disorientation, text legibility at distance, and haptic feedback absence — with design solutions for each
  • →Prototype used as a demonstration piece in the client's innovation showcase
  • →Established a reusable spatial UX methodology that the team applied to subsequent AR/VR engagements
