01

Brief

Kyndryl needed to enter the Indian banking and financial services sector — a market that is intensely relationship-driven and conservative about new IT infrastructure vendors. The brief was to understand not just the UX pain points, but the decision-making language of banking operations: what does "productivity" mean to a branch manager in Coimbatore, not just a CTO in Mumbai.

Kyndryl Vital was the product vehicle — an enterprise operations platform — and the research was both market intelligence and UX framing in one effort.

02

My role

Research strategy, coordination, and synthesis — I designed the study protocol and led the on-ground sessions across multiple states.

  • →Research study design and participant recruitment strategy
  • →On-site field interviews — bank branches, NBFC offices, IT ops centres
  • →Cross-functional synthesis sessions (Kyndryl sales, product, engineering)
  • →UX framing and narrative for the Kyndryl Vital banking pitch
03

The challenge

South Indian banking is not a monolith. The sector spans nationalised banks, cooperative banks, private sector institutions, and shadow-banking NBFCs — each with distinct IT procurement culture, regulatory pressure, and on-ground operational reality.

The challenge was to synthesise across this diversity and surface actionable insight — not generic "banking wants reliability" research, but specific friction points that Kyndryl's platform could credibly address.

04

What I drove

  • 01Designed a contextual inquiry protocol combining on-site observation with semi-structured interviews
  • 02Coordinated logistics across 5+ cities — Bangalore, Chennai, Coimbatore, Hyderabad, Mumbai
  • 03Led LUMA-facilitated synthesis workshops to identify patterns across participant segments
  • 04Built the research narrative that framed Kyndryl Vital's value proposition for Indian banking
  • 05Delivered findings to the Kyndryl sales and product leadership — directly informing the sector go-to-market
05

The outcome

  • →Research outcomes directly shaped the Kyndryl Vital banking pitch narrative
  • →Three NBFC and banking clients entered active sales conversations following the research-informed pitch
  • →Synthesis methodology was adopted as the standard for subsequent sector research within the Kyndryl UX practice
  • →Demonstrated that large-scale qualitative research is viable inside an infrastructure services company — a first for the practice
